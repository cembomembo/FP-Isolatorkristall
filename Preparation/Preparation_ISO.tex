\section{Introduction}

In this experiment we study the optical properties of a ruby crystal, which is aluminium oxide (Al$_2$O$_3$) doped with small amounts of trivalent chromium ions (Cr$^{3+}$).
These impurities give ruby its characteristic red colour and produce distinct absorption features in the visible spectrum.
By measuring the wavelength-dependent transmission of the crystal with a two-beam absorption spectrometer, we can identify the main absorption bands and relate them to electronic transitions of the Cr$^{3+}$ ions in the surrounding crystal field.
The aim of the experiment is to record the absorption spectrum of ruby, determine the positions of its characteristic bands, and use them to estimate the crystal-field parameter using the Tanabe–Sugano model.
