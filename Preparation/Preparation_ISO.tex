\section{Introduction}

In this experiment we investigate the optical properties of a ruby crystal, an aluminium oxide (Al$_2$O$_3$) crystal that appears red due to small amounts of Cr$^{3+}$ ions substituting for Al sites. These chromium impurities modify the electronic states of the ion through the surrounding crystal field and give rise to characteristic absorption features in the visible spectrum. By measuring the wavelength-dependent transmission of the ruby with a two-beam absorption spectrometer, we aim to identify these absorption bands and relate them to the electronic transitions responsible for the colour of ruby. The experiment therefore provides a first connection between optical spectroscopy and crystal-field considerations, which also play an important role in other doped insulating crystals such as Ti:sapphire or Nd:YAG.
